# ✈️ Fast and Fearless: Air India Meets McDonald's — Strategic Partnership Analysis

> **Strategic Management Case Study** | Hochschule Emden/Leer | June 2024

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents a **data-informed strategic consulting analysis** for a proposed partnership between **Air India** and **McDonald's**. As part of a consulting team, the goal was to evaluate whether this cross-industry collaboration could solve two real business problems simultaneously:

- Air India's stagnant international passenger bookings
- McDonald's need to reposition from fast food to premium food provider

The analysis follows a structured consulting framework using industry-standard strategic tools — the same analytical thinking that drives data-driven business decisions.

---

## 🔍 Business Problem (The Case)

Campbell Wilson (CEO, Air India, since 2022) faced a competitive crisis: international bookings were stagnant despite being India's most trusted airline. The CMO proposed an unconventional solution — partner with McDonald's to overhaul the in-flight dining experience.

**Key challenge:** Make this partnership viable across cultural, operational, brand, and regulatory dimensions — backed by evidence and structured analysis.

---

## 📊 Key Data Insights Used

- **Airport Traffic Data** (Statista): Delhi (6.52M) and Mumbai (3.18M) account for ~50% of India's international passenger volume — identified as priority pilot route hubs
- **Fast Food Brand Value Rankings** (Statista 2023): McDonald's brand value of $191B, a 310% gap over second-place Starbucks — strong co-branding leverage
- **Market Positioning**: McDonald's India success with McAloo Tikki (since 1998) demonstrates proven menu localization capability

---

## 📈 Strategic Recommendations (Data-Backed)

1. **Pilot Program** on Delhi–international routes (highest traffic volume)
2. **KPI Framework** defined: NPS scores, repeat passenger rates, cost-per-meal, sales growth on partnership routes
3. **Risk Prioritization**: Menu customization (High/High) and operational complexity (Medium/High) flagged as top risks
4. **Target**: 20% increase in positive brand sentiment for McDonald's; measurable uplift in Air India international bookings
